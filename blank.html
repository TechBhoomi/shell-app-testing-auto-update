Steps to Close Apps - Using Activity Monitor

  1. Open Activity Monitor:
    • Open Finder and navigate to the "Applications" folder.
    • Go to the "Utilities" folder and open "Activity Monitor".
    • Alternatively, use Spotlight Search by pressing Command + Space and typing "Activity Monitor".
  2. Select and Close the App:
    • Click on the app or process you want to close to select it.
    • Click the "X" button in the top-left corner of the Activity Monitor window.
    • A confirmation prompt may appear; click "Force Quit" to confirm and close the selected app.
  3. Verify Closure:
    • Check that the app has been closed by looking at the application window or by rechecking the Activity Monitor to confirm it's no longer listed among the running processes.

Steps to Close Apps - Using Terminal

  1. Open Terminal:
    • Open Finder and navigate to the "Applications" folder.
    • Go to the "Utilities" folder and open "Terminal".
    • Alternatively, use Spotlight Search by pressing Command + Space and typing "Terminal".
  2. Execute Terminal Commands to Close Apps:
    • Enter the following command to list running processes: 
      • ps aux
    • Find the process ID (PID) of the app you want to close. For example, to close Safari, you might find its PID by looking for "Safari" in the list.
    • Enter the following command to close the app: 
      • kill -9 PID
    • Replace "PID" with the actual process ID of the app you want to close.
    • Execute the command by pressing the Enter key.
    • Repeat the command with different process IDs to close multiple apps.
  3. Verify App Closure:
    • Check that the apps have been closed by verifying their absence in the Activity Monitor or by attempting to restart the closed apps.
